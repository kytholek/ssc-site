How to Calculate Your Life Path Number by Hand

June 2024 · 5 min read

The SSC calculator does this automatically — but understanding the manual process matters. It removes the black box, reveals the logic, and gives you the ability to verify results and explain the method to others.

The Reduction Method

The core operation in Pythagorean numerology is reduction: adding the digits of a number together until you reach a single digit (or a master number). 28 becomes 2+8=10, then 1+0=1. 38 becomes 3+8=11 — stop here, this is a master number. 47 becomes 4+7=11 — again, stop.

Calculating the Life Path

Take your full birth date. Write out each digit. Add them all together. Reduce to a root number or master number.

Example: Born 14 March 1988. The digits are 1, 4, 3, 1, 9, 8, 8. Sum: 1+4+3+1+9+8+8 = 34. Reduce: 3+4 = 7. Life Path: 7.

Example with master number: Born 29 November 1975. Digits: 2, 9, 1, 1, 1, 9, 7, 5. Sum: 35. Reduce: 3+5 = 8. Life Path: 8.

The calculation is simple. The interpretation is where the depth lives — but you cannot interpret accurately without calculating correctly first.

Common Mistakes

The most frequent error is stopping the reduction too early or continuing it past a master number. Remember: reduce to a single digit unless you reach 11, 22, or 33 — at which point you stop. A result of 22 is a master number. A result of 44 is not — reduce to 8.

The second common error is using the wrong birth date — a transcription error, a date that has been remembered incorrectly, or a date of adoption rather than birth. When a reading does not resonate, this is the first thing to check.
