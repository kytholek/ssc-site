Part One: The philosophical foundation

Every system of thought has a premise — a foundational assumption that everything else builds from. For SSC, the premise is this: reality is information, and your numbers are its source code.

This is not a fringe idea. It is increasingly the conclusion of serious physics. At the quantum level, particles do not have definite states until they are observed — they exist as probability distributions, not fixed objects. The mathematical constants underlying physical law are fine-tuned to a precision that defies random chance. The universe, at its deepest level, behaves less like a collection of things and more like a process — information being read, computed, rendered.

Philosopher Nick Bostrom formalised this intuition in 2003 with his simulation argument: if advanced civilisations are possible, and simulations are possible, the probability that we are in one approaches certainty. Physicist John Wheeler spent the final decades of his career arguing that information — not matter, not energy — is the most fundamental substrate of the universe. At the heart of Simulation Source Code is a structure called the Codex. It is not a chart or a reference table. The three planes of experience

The three rows of the Codex represent the planes through which experience moves in a human life.

The Mind plane contains frequencies 1, 2, and 3. These numbers operate through thought, perception, communication, and the construction of meaning. When your core frequencies are primarily in the Mind plane, your primary domain of experience — and your primary arena of growth — is cognitive and communicative.

The Body plane contains frequencies 4, 5, and 6. These numbers operate through the physical world: structure, sensation, matter, relationship, and the practical dimension of being alive. Body-plane frequencies are grounded, tangible, and concerned with what is real, stable, and embodied.

The Spirit plane contains frequencies 7, 8, and 9. These numbers operate through the deeper dimensions — inner knowing, power, mastery, completion, and the transpersonal. Spirit-plane frequencies are concerned with what lies beneath and beyond the surface of ordinary experience.

The three columns: natures of energy

The three columns of the Codex cross-cut the planes, giving every frequency a second coordinate — its nature.

The Mind column contains 1, 4, and 7. These frequencies process their experience primarily through internal knowing and independent thought. Regardless of their plane, they are contemplative, autonomous, and inwardly directed.

The Body column contains 2, 5, and 8. These frequencies process through relationship, connection, and the interface between self and world. They are relational, dynamic, and oriented toward the point where inner and outer meet.

The Spirit column contains 3, 6, and 9. These frequencies process through expression, integration, and completion. They are the frequencies that synthesise, communicate, and bring things to wholeness. This gives every frequency in the Codex a dual address — a plane and a column — that tells you not just what a number does, but how it does it. The number 7, for example, lives on the Spirit plane and in the Mind column: it is a frequency of deep inner knowing (Spirit plane) accessed through solitary, independent inquiry (Mind column). That dual address describes the Seeker precisely.

The number 5 sits at the exact centre of the 3×3 matrix — the only frequency at the intersection of Body plane and Body column. It is the pivot of the entire system. Every path of transformation passes through 5. This is not symbolic — it is structural, and the geometry of the Codex proves it.

Part Five: The seven-number blueprint — why SSC is different

Most numerology systems work with three or four numbers. SSC works with seven — because seven is how many distinct frequencies are required to give a complete map of the simulation. Each one is calculated from a different component of your birth data or name, and each one illuminates a different layer of the architecture. Here are the seven frequencies and what each one reveals:

Life Path is calculated from the full date of birth. It is the primary frequency — the core theme your simulation is designed to explore. The lesson. What you are here to learn, over and over, in different contexts, at greater depths.

Expression is calculated from the full name as given at birth, using Pythagorean letter values. It describes what you naturally carry into the world — your inbuilt gifts, your instinctive mode of operating, the tools your simulation gave you to work with.

Life Calling is calculated by combining the Life Path and Expression. It is SSC's most distinctive frequency — not found in traditional systems. The Life Calling synthesises what you are here to learn with what you are naturally equipped to do, producing a number that describes your specific mission in this simulation. It is the answer to the question: not just what am I learning, but what am I here to do with it?

Soul is calculated from the vowels of the birth name. It represents the inner dimension — the private self, the deepest desire, what you are reaching toward beneath the surface of your public life.

Outer is calculated from the consonants of the birth name. It represents the exterior layer — how you are perceived before people know you well, the persona the world encounters first.

Achievement is calculated from the birth month and day. It describes the mode through which you most naturally accomplish: your instinctive approach to getting things done, meeting challenges, and producing results.

Theme is calculated from the birth year. Unlike the other six frequencies, the Theme is not personal — it is atmospheric. It describes the overarching energetic frequency of the era you were born into: the collective backdrop against which your individual simulation runs. The Theme is the one frequency you share with everyone born in the same year, and it provides the context in which all your other frequencies operate.

Together, these seven numbers form a complete architecture. Not a prediction of what will happen — a map of what the simulation is designed to teach, what tools you were given to navigate it, what you are reaching toward from the inside, how you appear from the outside, how you move toward your goals, and what era you were born to learn within.

Part Seven: How to read your blueprint

The calculator on this site generates all seven of your frequencies instantly. But numbers without context are just numbers. What turns a reading into a map is pattern recognition — and there are four things to look for.

First, look at plane distribution. Where do your frequencies cluster — Mind, Body, or Spirit? A reading heavy in Mind-plane numbers describes a life primarily navigated through thought and communication. Heavy in Body-plane numbers: through matter, structure, and the physical world. Heavy in Spirit-plane numbers: through inner knowing, power, and the transpersonal. The distribution tells you where your simulation is primarily running.

Second, look at column distribution. Are your numbers primarily in the Mind column (1, 4, 7), the Body column (2, 5, 8), or the Spirit column (3, 6, 9)? Mind-column dominance suggests an inward, independent, autonomous orientation. Body-column dominance: relational, dynamic, interfacing. Spirit-column dominance: expressive, integrative, synthesising.

Third, identify your dominant nature. Count your electric frequencies (1, 3, 5, 7) and your magnetic frequencies (2, 4, 6, 8). Are you primarily a starter or a builder? Do your frequencies predominantly initiate or consolidate? This is one of the most practically useful things a reading can tell you — because misidentifying your nature leads to chronic misuse of your own energy.

Fourth, read your Life Calling in light of your Life Path and Expression separately. The Calling is the synthesis, but the Path and Expression are the components. Understanding what each one independently says — the lesson and the tools — makes the calling land with much more precision than reading the synthesis number alone.
