First: The Numbers That Are Missing

Before examining what the loop contains, notice what it does not. The sequence passes through seven of the nine numbers � 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 8, 9 � and returns to 5 and 1 to close the circuit. Two numbers are entirely absent.

4 and 6.

In the Codex, 4 and 6 occupy the Body row alongside 5 � but they represent something specific. Position 4 is structure, discipline, the container built to hold growth. Position 6 is integration, the responsible application of understanding to behaviour. Both are essential for development. But both carry the same shadow: they can calcify. Four mistakes the structure for the thing it was built to serve. Six uses responsibility as a mechanism for control rather than care.

The book identifies 4 and 6 as the Physical Fixation positions � the numbers that mistake form for function, that confuse the vehicle with the destination. The infinity loop deliberately skips them both. Not because they are unimportant, but because the living circuit of consciousness � the arc of the soul in motion � does not pass through the positions that arrest movement. The loop is the current. Four and six are the banks of the river, not the water.

The Three Rows as a Single Arc

And the loop that traces the journey of consciousness through all its stages is, when reduced to a single frequency, nothing other than the frequency of that passage itself.

The infinity loop is not a symbol of endless repetition. It is a symbol of endless refinement � the same circuit, run at ever-higher resolution, consciousness becoming more precise in its translation of light into lived experience.

What the Sigil Is Actually Showing You

The Codex Sigil presents two geometric realities simultaneously. The static structure � the cross, the nodes, the outer ring � maps the architecture of consciousness as it exists at any given moment. The infinity loop traces the path consciousness takes through that architecture over the course of a life.

The loop begins at initiation (1). It traverses the entire Mind row � developing perception, relationship, and meaning. Then it drops through the body (5) without stopping to build structure first � because structure without contact with reality produces rigidity, not growth. It ascends through the Spirit row � developing self-knowledge, embodied authority, and completion. Then it passes back through the body (5) before returning to 1, because wisdom that does not re-enter the world is not yet complete.

The two absent numbers � 4 and 6 � are the shadow. Not because they are bad, but because they represent the ways the circuit can get arrested. You can stop at 4 and spend a lifetime perfecting your systems without ever testing them. You can stop at 6 and spend a lifetime serving others without ever completing your own loop. The sigil does not include them in the living circuit because the living circuit requires movement, and movement is what 4 and 6, in their shadow expressions, most resist.

The loop is the medicine for that arrest. It shows you the way through.

That self-knowledge consolidated into a change in how you carry yourself in the world (8). Something completed and was released (9). And you returned to the body (5) and the new beginning (1) with more precision than before.

They are not telling you who you are in a fixed sense.
