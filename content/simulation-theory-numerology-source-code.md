    You Are Running on a Simulation — And Your Numbers Are the Source Code
    December 2024 — 8 min read
    
      
In 2003, philosopher Nick Bostrom published a paper that sent a quiet tremor through academic circles. His argument, simply stated: at least one of three things must be true. Either civilisations almost always go extinct before becoming technologically mature. Or technologically mature civilisations almost never run simulations of their ancestors. Or we are almost certainly living in a computer simulation right now.

Physicists and mathematicians have taken the idea seriously ever since. Not as science fiction — as a testable hypothesis. The evidence is not conclusive, but it is uncomfortable. The universe runs on mathematics with uncanny precision. Physical constants appear fine-tuned. Reality at the quantum level behaves like information being processed, not matter existing independently.

### If Reality Is Computational, Then What Are Numbers?

Here is the question that animates Simulation Source Code: if the universe is fundamentally mathematical — if it is, at some level, a computation — then numbers are not symbols. They are not human constructs projected onto a neutral reality. They are the structure of reality itself.

Your birth date is not a random accident. It is a timestamp — a specific coordinate in the computational process. Your name, assigned at birth, carries a numerical signature. These are not poetic metaphors. They are literally the parameters of your instantiation.

> "The universe is written in the language of mathematics." — Galileo Galilei, 1623

Galileo said this four centuries before simulation theory. He was describing something he had observed in the behaviour of falling bodies and planetary orbits. The universe does not merely contain mathematics. The universe **is** mathematics.

### What Numerology Actually Is

Understood through this lens, numerology is not mysticism. It is a discipline for reading the mathematical parameters of a given life. The Pythagorean system — which SSC uses — assigns values to letters and reduces all numbers to their root frequency. This is not arbitrary: it is a coherent framework for translating the information in names and dates into legible numerical patterns.

The patterns are real. Across thousands of readings, the correlations between life path numbers and the actual themes of people's lives are not coincidental. The numbers describe something genuine — not because numbers are magic, but because numbers are the substrate of the reality in which we operate.

### How to Use This

Understanding your numbers does not tell you what will happen. A simulation can be run many ways from the same starting parameters, depending on the choices made at each branch point. What your numbers reveal is the **architecture** — the terrain, the tendencies, the recurring patterns that show up in your life regardless of the specific events.

That is enormously useful information. Not as fate. As a map.
